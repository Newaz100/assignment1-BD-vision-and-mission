Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh has been fostering innovation through the development of ICT industries, the promotion of startups, and government initiatives like the "Digital Bangladesh" vision, which aims to integrate technology into various sectors.

Sustainability

The country is adopting sustainable practices, such as renewable energy projects like solar home systems in rural areas, and focusing on climate resilience due to its vulnerability to environmental changes.

Infrastructure

Significant progress in infrastructure development includes projects like the Padma Bridge, metro rail in Dhaka, and the expansion of highways to improve connectivity and economic growth.

Empowerment

Bangladesh has achieved notable success in empowering women through initiatives like microfinance (Grameen Bank), education programs, and high female participation in the garment sector.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation's trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Muhammad Yunus and Grameen Bank were awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for 2006 for their work to “create economic and social development from below”. Grameen Bank's objective since its establishment in 1983 has been to grant poor people small loans on easy terms - so-called micro-credit - and Yunus was the bank's founder. "Poverty is not created by the poor. It is created by the system we have built, the institutions we have designed, and the concepts we have formulated." This quote emphasizes that poverty is not an inherent condition or a personal failing of individuals. Instead, it is the result of structural issues like flawed policies, unequal opportunities, and unfair economic systems. Dr. Yunus argues that systemic barriers—such as lack of access to education, healthcare, and financial services—trap people in poverty. To eliminate poverty, he advocates for transforming these systems to ensure fairness, inclusivity, and access to resources for all.
